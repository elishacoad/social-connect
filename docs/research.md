# Research

## The Core Bet

Does the fading/reconnection mechanic emotionally resonate without feeling manipulative?

Everything else is secondary. If this doesn't work, nothing else matters.

## What to Validate First

Not "would users post?" but:

1. Does fading feel meaningful, not stressful?
2. Does reconnecting feel satisfying?
3. Would people want close friends on this?
4. Would they actually use it?
5. Does it feel different enough from existing apps?

## How to Validate

### Phase 1: Prototype the Emotional Loop

Before building much, manually prototype the emotional loop:

- Figma prototype with fake feeds, fake fading, fake reconnecting
- Watch people react emotionally
- This is WAY more valuable than polishing infrastructure early

### Phase 2: Experience Walkthroughs

Narrate scenarios together:

> "You haven't seen a college friend in months. You run into them at a coffee shop. What happens in the app?"

Walk through emotional state, actions, UI, reactions, aftermath. This exposes awkwardness, friction, and emotional mismatch faster than coding.

### Phase 3: User Interviews

**Do NOT pitch the app first.**

#### Current Behavior Questions

- "How do you currently keep up with close friends?"
- "Who do you wish you stayed closer with?"
- "What happens to friendships when life gets busy?"
- "How often do you reconnect with people intentionally?"

#### Frustration Discovery

- "What do you dislike about existing social apps?"
- "Do you feel social media makes you feel more connected or less?"
- "Do you ever feel pressure to perform online?"

#### Existing Workarounds

- "Have you ever tried to intentionally maintain friendships?"
- "Do you use reminders / group chats / etc?"
- "Do you mostly lurk or actively post?"

#### Concept Reaction (after behavior discussion)

"What if there was an app focused on maintaining close friendships through small moments and real-world reconnection?"

Then watch reactions carefully.

### Phase 4: Build and Test MVP

Test with friends. Focus on emotional reactions. Validate behavior, not opinions.

## What Metrics Matter

### Do NOT Optimize For

- Screen time
- Post count
- DAU at all costs

### Strong Signals

- People reconnecting physically
- Replies per moment
- Friendships sustained over months
- Users adding close friends
- Emotional reactions in interviews
- Retention among small friend clusters

### The Strongest Validation Signal

If users say:

> "This made me think about people differently."

or:

> "I actually reached out to someone because of this."

That's massive. The product is fundamentally behavior-shaping social infrastructure, not entertainment.

## Risk Map

| Risk | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| Fading feels punitive or stressful | Core mechanic backfires emotionally |
| People won't post daily | Core loop breaks |
| Reconnecting feels awkward IRL | Bump ritual doesn't land |
| Not different enough from BeReal | No reason to switch |
| Cold start / empty feed | No value without friends on app |
| Becomes just another chat app | Loses identity if messaging grows |

## Key Principle

Validate the behavior, not the idea. People saying "cool idea" means almost nothing. You need to test: will people actually change behavior?

- Open app consistently?
- Reconnect physically?
- Share low-friction moments?
- Use app instead of passive scrolling?
