# Vision

## One-Liner

A private social app for maintaining real friendships through lightweight moments and real-world reconnection.

## Core Philosophy

**Capture a moment, then go live it.**

Marketing tagline: **Social media for people who hate social media**

The app should work _for_ the user, not against them. Success means less compulsive usage, more meaningful relationships, more real-world interaction, and deeper friendships.

## What We Believe

- Relationships are living, temporal, dynamic, and seasonal
- They strengthen through presence and drift through absence
- They reconnect naturally
- Real life matters more than app engagement
- Presence matters more than performance
- Relationships matter more than audiences
- Moments matter more than content
- Intentionality matters more than infinite consumption
- Softness matters more than gamification

## The Problem

Instead of connecting people, social media often isolates them. It creates self-consciousness and depression. The attention economy, algorithmic feeds, inauthenticity, and posting friction all work against genuine human connection.

People don't want "a new social media app." They want to stay meaningfully connected to the people they care about without the pressure and performative nature of traditional social media.

## What This App Is

- Friendship infrastructure
- A social ritual
- Warm, calm, human, fleeting, intentional, low-pressure

## What This App Is Not

- A content platform
- A creator economy
- A messaging app
- An entertainment product

## Anti-Goals

- Should never feel addictive
- Should never feel like LinkedIn networking
- Should never reward posting frequency
- Should never create streak anxiety
- Should never become influencer-oriented
- Should never use algorithms to select content
- Should never extract attention

## Jobs To Be Done

### Primary Job

"Help me maintain meaningful relationships without the pressure and performative nature of traditional social media."

### Supporting Jobs

- "Help me stay meaningfully connected to people I care about."
- "Help me stay present in my friends' lives."
- "Help me maintain friendships despite busy adult life."
- "Help me share small moments without turning them into content."
- "Help me connect without getting sucked into infinite feeds."
- "Help me participate socially without becoming a content creator."

### What People Currently "Hire" for These Jobs

- Instagram stories
- Snapchat streaks
- Group chats
- BeReal
- Texting friends occasionally
- Calendar reminders to reconnect
- "Thinking about someone but never reaching out"

### Where Current Solutions Fail

- They optimize for engagement over connection
- They create performance pressure
- They reward passive consumption
- They make relationships feel transactional
- They don't encourage real-world interaction

## Emotional Thesis

The desired emotions and behaviors:

- Feeling "done" with the app quickly
- Check in, see new moments, then leave
- Quick and fun interaction
- Feel more connected and maintain meaningful relationships
- Encourage conviction, vulnerability, expression
- Avoid discouragement or pressure
