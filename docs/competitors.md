# Competitors

## Landscape

A cultural shift is underway away from algorithmic, ad-driven social media. 1 in 4 consumers deleted a social app in the past year (1 in 3 for Gen Z). The "real friends only" space is attracting serious VC money ($50M+ across new entrants) but retention remains the core unsolved problem.

## Direct Competitors

### Friendster (2026 relaunch)

- **URL:** https://friendster.com/
- **What it is:** The closest competitor to our vision. Users must physically tap phones together (NFC) to add friends. No friend search, no suggestions, no remote adding.
- **Key mechanics:**
  - NFC-only connection via Apple Core NFC (~4 cm range)
  - Relationship decay if users don't tap again within a year
  - Friends-of-friends online connection (only if a mutual friend has tapped with both)
  - No ads, no algorithms, no public follower counts, no data sales
- **Status:** iOS only, launched April 2026. Very early. Founded by Mike Carson (Park.io). Domain acquired for $30K.
- **Where they differ from us:** No moments/feed concept visible yet. Decay is binary (1-year timer) vs. our weighted decay. No lightweight content sharing layer.
- **Risk level:** Low for now (iOS-only, just launched), but the brand recognition of "Friendster" gives them attention.

## Close-Friends / Small-Circle Apps

### Locket

- **URL:** https://locket.camera/
- **What it is:** Friends send photos that appear directly on your home screen widget. Capped at 20 friends max.
- **Relevance:** Proves that small-circle, low-friction photo sharing resonates with Gen Z. Widget-first UX is interesting. No feed scrolling.

### BeReal

- **URL:** https://bereal.com/
- **What it is:** Daily simultaneous photo prompt (front + back camera, 2-minute window). Anti-curation.
- **Relevance:** Proved demand for authenticity, but retention collapsed. Acquired by Voodoo for ~$500M. Cautionary tale: novelty mechanics need emotional depth to sustain.

### Whee (ByteDance)

- **What it is:** Instagram-like photo sharing visible only to close friends. No ads currently.
- **Relevance:** ByteDance resources behind a close-friends play. Available in ~12 countries, not yet US.

### Favs

- **What it is:** Private-by-default social network. No public profiles, no ads, no tracking.
- **Relevance:** Similar privacy-first positioning but no physical connection mechanic.

## No-Ads / No-Algorithm Platforms

### Vero

- **URL:** https://vero.co/
- **What it is:** Chronological feed, no ads, no algorithms. Users categorize contacts (close friends, friends, acquaintances, followers) and share different content to each group.
- **Relevance:** Planned subscription model is a proof point for paid social. Relationship-tier sharing is interesting.

### Noplace

- **What it is:** Text-only social network with MySpace-style customizable profiles. Reverse chronological. Hit #1 on App Store at launch.
- **Relevance:** Proves Gen Z appetite for anti-algorithm, personality-driven social. No photo/video focus.

## Real-Life Meetup / Proximity

### Jagat

- **What it is:** Location-based social map. Friends appear as avatars on a real-time map. 10M+ users.
- **Relevance:** Largest player in the "real-life connection" space. Map-first, not content-first.

### Pie

- **What it is:** AI matches people weekly for real-life events. Founded by Andy Dunn (Bonobos). 130K MAUs, $11.5M raised.
- **Relevance:** Different goal (meeting new people vs. maintaining existing friendships) but validates IRL-first social.

## Notable Failure

### Maven

- Backed by Evan Williams (Twitter) and Sam Altman (OpenAI). $2M seed.
- Designed explicitly against dopamine hits but failed to sustain growth for follow-on funding.
- **Lesson:** Anti-engagement positioning alone doesn't solve retention. The emotional loop has to be genuinely satisfying, not just "less bad."

## What This Means for Us

- **Our differentiator is the combination:** physical reconnection + weighted relationship decay + lightweight moments + no-algorithm feed. No one app does all of these together.
- **Friendster is closest** but lacks a content/moments layer and uses binary decay.
- **Retention is the industry's unsolved problem.** Our weighted decay mechanic (physical > direct > passive) is our bet on making the core loop emotionally sustainable, not just novel.
- **Avoid BeReal's trap:** a gimmick mechanic that's fun once but doesn't deepen over time.
